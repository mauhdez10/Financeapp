## 6. Objection handling — five real answers

**1. "I can't afford a financial advisor."**
You can afford this one. The app is free forever, Premium starts at $3 a month — the
price of a coffee — and you pick the amount. The insurance consultation never costs
anything. We built the pricing for working families, not for the wealthy.

**2. "I don't trust apps with my money / I've been burned before."** *(notario fraud,
pushy salespeople)*
Good — be careful. Here's why we're different: we never touch your money, never ask for
your bank login, and never sell investments. When we refer you to someone (a realtor,
a lender), we tell you exactly what our relationship with them is. And you're dealing
with a licensed professional with a name and a license number you can verify, not an
anonymous app.

**3. "I'm not good with technology."**
Neither is half our community — that's who we built it for. Big clear numbers, plain
words, simple steps. And if the app ever confuses you, you have a real person to call
who will walk you through it in your language. You're never alone with a screen.

**4. "My bank / a free nonprofit already offers financial advice."**
Those are good resources and we'll never tell you to avoid them. The difference: banks
advise you toward their products, and nonprofit counseling is usually one conversation
about one problem (often only in English). We stay with you — same advisor, your whole
picture, both languages, month after month.

**5. "Is this investment advice? Are you going to push me into stocks?"**
No, and that's a promise written into how we operate. We are educators and coaches —
budgeting, debt, savings, goals, insurance. We never recommend specific investments and
never manage money. If you ever need an investment professional, we refer you to a
licensed one and disclose any relationship. That boundary protects you, and it's why
you can trust the rest of what we say.
